# Vision

The thesis first, then the horizons. The nearer the horizon, the more provable the
claim; the farther, the more it is a direction we commit to rather than a thing
already built. We try to be honest about which is which everywhere else in this
repository, and we hold to that here too.

## The thesis — the soul-bound entity

A person's digital existence should be **non-transferable from their living body** —
an identity that cannot be stolen because it is not a possession. From that one idea
everything else follows: presence gates the keys; property binds to the person, not
to a credential; separate contexts of a life stay unlinkable; and what is bound to
the living *you* cannot be extracted from a coerced you. Atlas is that idea expressed
at every layer.

This is the load-bearing invariant: **the living signal times and gates the
cryptography — it never enters a key or value.** Biology times; randomness values.
Liveness proves a person is *alive and present*, never *who* they are, and Atlas
keeps no biometric template.

## Today — the proof

The mechanism works, and you can check it. An open-source, post-quantum trust layer,
tested across two implementations with continuous integration, run end-to-end on
physical phones with commodity wearables — keys that live only while a person is
present, verified by the signal's own coherence, never by a stored profile. The
claim today is deliberately narrow because it is the one we can prove: *the mechanism
works, on hardware anyone can afford.*

## 5 years — the utility

"Verified live human" becomes a service any platform can consume — and none can
abuse. Atlas as the authenticator behind the places where humanness now matters
most: proving works are human-made, replacing bot-filters with presence, holding
Sybil-free votes — consumed over protocols services already speak (passkeys), with
no biometric database anywhere. Externally audited, formally analyzed, running from
a bare phone up to instrumented wearables — the same guarantee at every price point.
The test of this horizon is adoption under audit: *a stranger's platform trusting a
stranger's presence, through math neither controls.*

## 20 years — the substrate

Presence-bound identity becomes infrastructure — the quiet layer beneath services,
the way encryption became the quiet layer beneath commerce. In a world thick with
synthetic actors, the one primitive machines cannot mint is *a living person is here,
once, and their contexts don't link.* Systems that today demand a whole identity
learn to ask only the one fact they need. This is where the authenticator becomes a
**property substrate**: the binding layer between a living person and everything they
own or control — fiat, digital assets, records, title — where the registry keeps its
job and Atlas supplies the presence check it never had. Property as an extension of
the person, unbroken from the real world into the digital one. The test of this
horizon is invisibility: *people stop being asked to surrender anything to prove they
are here.*

## 100 years — the invariant

Wherever there are people, a person can prove they are one — and cannot be made into
less than one. Not a product; a property of civilization we intend to make permanent:
that aliveness and presence stay provable, private, and unownable — by any company,
including this one, and by any state. That the poorest person's proof is as strong as
the richest one's. That coercion leaves evidence and anonymity survives scrutiny. The
governance structure ([`GOVERNANCE.md`](GOVERNANCE.md)) — the foundation, the open
license, the pledge — exists so that this outlasts its founders: by then, *nobody has
to trust Clockwork Tree for any of it to remain true.*

---

The same invariant runs all the way up. The protocol binds property to the person who
*is* it; the commons binds returns to the people who *make* it. Soul-bound, every
layer.
