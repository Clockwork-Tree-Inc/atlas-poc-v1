# Governance & the IP Commons

How Atlas is owned, funded, and kept a public good. This document states what is
**true today** and what Clockwork Tree Inc. has **committed to** — and is careful
to mark which is which.

---

## Today (in force now)

- **Atlas is developed by Clockwork Tree Inc.**, which holds the patent (a filed
  provisional; inventor **Aun Ali**) and the copyright in this repository.
- **The reference implementation is open source under AGPL-3.0.** Anyone may run,
  study, modify, and share it under those terms. Nothing in the commitments below
  can be used to *narrow* what AGPL-3.0 already grants — it can only widen it.
- **Contributors sign a CLA** (Apache ICLA). This lets the project maintain a
  single clean licensing point. See [`CLA.md`](CLA.md) and
  [`CONTRIBUTING.md`](CONTRIBUTING.md) — including, in CONTRIBUTING, the
  reciprocity commitment below written in plain terms *before* you sign.
- **AGPL-3.0 already carries a patent license (§11).** By publishing this code
  under AGPL, Clockwork Tree has already granted every user a royalty-free patent
  license for the essential claims practiced by the published implementation. That
  grant is irrevocable for released versions.

## Committed (the direction, stated so it can be held to)

These are commitments, not yet-executed legal instruments. They are written here
publicly so that they are costly to walk back, and so that early contributors and
partners know the structure they are joining.

1. **A free, irrevocable, perpetual license to a nonprofit foundation.** Clockwork
   Tree will grant a foundation (being established) an unconditional license over
   the core protocol for the open implementation — so the core can never be closed,
   restricted, or made pay-to-use, regardless of what happens to the company. The
   license will be a **separate instrument from any revenue arrangement**, so it
   survives even if the company does not.
2. **A defined share of commercial revenue funds the foundation.** The company
   funds the commons from a fixed share of gross commercial revenue (the exact
   figure to be set with the foundation at its formation, and to **bind successors
   and assigns** so an acquisition cannot launder it away). The foundation is meant
   to be *resourced*, not merely permitted.
3. **Contributors become members of the commons.** Through the foundation,
   contributors gain a governance voice and a share of the returns their work
   creates — via grants, bounties, and paid roles. Returns are **fiat, grants, and
   employment — never tokens** (a token path, if the designed economy layer ever
   matures under proper legal review, is a separate future question and is promised
   to no one here).
4. **A patent non-assertion pledge** for the open implementation, drafted with
   defensive-termination terms (anyone who sues the ecosystem over patents loses
   the benefit), will be published as these instruments are formalized.

## Why it is built this way

The **company** builds and sells the applications, hardware, and integrations that
fund the work — and pays the people who do it, including the founder. The
**foundation** guarantees the protocol stays a public good, surviving the founders,
investors, or any acquisition. In one line: **we give away the invariant, and we
sell the labor.**

This is a recognized structure — Signal (Foundation over Messenger LLC), Mozilla
(Foundation over Corporation), Blender (Development Fund paying contributors to work
on the commons), Godot (Foundation alongside a commercial arm). Atlas applies it
with a patent held in the trunk and pledged to the commons.

## Status of this document

This is a **charter of intent**, not the operating agreement of a foundation that
exists yet. When the foundation is established, the binding instruments (license,
revenue covenant, membership, pledge) will be executed and linked here, and this
document will be updated to move each item from *Committed* to *In force*. Until
then, the honest status is: company holds the IP, AGPL-3.0 and its §11 patent grant
are live, and the commitments above are public and on the record.
